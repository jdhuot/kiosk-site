Francise Clarke

Major • Calgary Highlanders • 1939 – 1945

Profile: Francis Herbert "Nobby" Clarke joined the Calgary Highlanders Non-Permanent Active Militia in 1939 and went overseas in 1942 as a lieutenant. He served with the Highlanders in North-West Europe and was wounded on 20 July 1944 when he received a head wound at Hill 67 near Caen. Lt.Col. Clarke received the Distinguished Service Order (DSO) on 7 November 1945 for his combat work as an officer, in particular for his "indifference to danger; cool and aggressive leadership; and sound judgement (sic)". He completed his wartime service with the rank of major, and continued to serve with the Highlanders in peacetime, as the president of the Highlanders Association and eventually became commander of the infantry militia as lieutenant-colonel in 1959. LCol Clarke died at the age of 79 on 19 August 1993.

 

Years of service: 1939 – 1945
