Ralph MacLean

Rifleman • Royal Rifles of Canada • 1941 – 1945

Profile: Ralph MacLean grew up in the Magdalen Islands in the Gulf of St. Lawrence. In the clips here, he is one of three survivors of the battle of Hong Kong in 1941. He and Douglas Reids and John Deer were some of the first Canadians to see action in Asia during the war. He was a prisoner of the Japanese until 1945. After the war, his daughter married a Japanese-Canadian man whose parents had been internees in Canada. His grandson (Mark Sakamoto) wrote a book about the two families called “Forgiveness”.

 

Years of service: 1940 – 1941, POW until 1945
