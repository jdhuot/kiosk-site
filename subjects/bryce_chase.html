Bryce Chase

Pilot • Royal Canadian Air Force • 1941 – 1945

Profile: Born on June 6, 1923 in North Battleford, Saskatchewan. Bryce would join the RCAF just after he turned 18 in 1941 and eventually trained at the Elementary Flying School in Edmonton, piloting Tiger Moth biplanes for a year.   In 1943 he was redeployed to Burma where he flew DC-3 Dakota cargo planes, dropping supplies to Allied armies advancing through the jungle. After the war, Bryce went back to civilian life and graduated from university in leadership and counselling. He was also a member of the Burma Star veterans association.

 

Years of service: 1941 – 1945
