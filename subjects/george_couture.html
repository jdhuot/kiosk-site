George Couture

Private • Royal Winnipeg Rifles • 1942 – 1945

Profile: George grew up in Selkirk, Manitoba, with deep family roots in New France (originally from Normandy) dating to the 17th century. In 1942, he enlisted in the Canadian Army in Winnipeg. On June 6, 1944 (D-Day) he landed at Courselle-Sur-Mer (Juno Beach) France with the Royal Winnipeg Rifles - bringing his family full circle liberating his ancestral homeland. Two days later he was wounded and captured by the Germans. He was then sent to Krakow, Poland as a Prisoner of War (POW). He was finally liberated by the Allied Forces in late April, 1945.

 

Years of service: 1941 – 1945
