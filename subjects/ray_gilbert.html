Ray Gilbert

Maintenance Officer • Calgary Tank Regiment • 1942 – 1946

Profile: Born in Stettler Alberta, Ray Gilbert was a butcher before joining the militia at the age of 16. He then joined the Calgary Regiment and trained as a tank radio-operator/loader. He went ashore during the Dieppe Raid on August 19th 1942. He surrendered and became a prisoner of war in Germany. Many of his accounts are also featured in the Dieppe/POW exhibit in the King’s Own Calgary Regiment (RCAC) gallery.

 

Years of service: 1942 – 1945
